\chapter{Grammar Tools}
\label{cha:grammar_tools}

The tools for FLAT aimed at automata can be classified into two major categories:
text-based automaton simulators and graphical interface-based automaton simulators. 
The first involves specifying a model through structured text, which must be processed using a parser, the second uses a graphical 
interface that allows creating, editing, and using an automaton to recognize words

The different existing tools can also support only one type of automaton or multiple types of automata, 
such as finite automata, pushdown automata, and Turing machines.
There are also tools that deal with regular expressions and grammars, and in such cases, it is 
common for conversion operations to be available between automata and vice versa.

Next, some pedagogical tools are presented and classified according to the indicators described above, i.e., 
whether they are text-based tools or graphical interface-based tools; whether they implement only one type of 
automaton or multiple types, and whether they support regular expressions and grammars.
We will discuss three such tools: \textbf{JFLAT}, \textbf{ANTLR}, and \textbf{CGM}.
\section{JFLAT}

JFLAT (Java Formal Languages and Automata Toolkit) is a versatile tool designed for experimenting with formal languages, 
it supports various computational models, including finite automata, pushdown automata, Turing machines, and different types 
of grammars this tool also provides a comprehensive environment for users to explore and understand the theoretical foundations 
of computer science. JFLAT offers several key features that enhance its utility as an educational tool as it allows users to create 
and visualize the computational models helping students better understand the structure and behaviour of the same models. This tool 
supports the conversion between different forms of automata. Additionally, JFLAT includes parsing capabilities 
for various types of grammars, enabling users to experiment with context-free grammars and other formal language constructs.

JFLAT also provides educational benefits by offering a visual and interactive approach to learning complex concepts, 
as it have the ability to visualize automata and grammars helping users to gain a deeper understanding of theoretical 
concepts through hands-on experimentation, develop problem-solving skills by working through practical exercises and projects. 
Examples of exercises and projects include creating and testing finite automata to recognize specific patterns or languages, 
designing and parsing context-free grammars to explore the syntax and structure of programming languages.

\section{ANTLR}

ANTLR (ANother Tool for Language Recognition) is a parser generator used for reading, processing, and 
translating structured text or binary files, it offers several notable features, including the ability to generate parsers, 
lexers, and tree parsers from grammars, facilitating the creation of language processors. It  also supports multiple programming 
languages, making it versatile for various development environments and also can be used to build languages, interpreters, compilers, 
and other language-related frameworks.

This tool helps users understand the principles of syntax analysis and parsing, building parsers and compilers, and exploring the language 
design and implementation. ANTLR can be used in various educational projects and assignments, such as creating parsers for custom programming 
languages, building simple compilers to learn about the different stages of compilation, and developing domain-specific languages
to appreciate the practical applications of the tool in real-world scenarios.

\section{CGM: Context-Free Grammar Manipulator}

CGM (Context-Free Grammar Manipulator) is a tool developed at the University of Porto designed to assist in the manipulation and 
study of context-free grammars. It provides a graphical environment for editing grammars, converting them to Chomsky normal form, 
parsing words, and constructing parse trees interactively, it offers several key functionalities, including grammar editing, Chomsky 
normal form conversion, and the ability to parse words and visualize the corresponding parse trees, aiding in the understanding of grammar 
structures.

CGM helps users to visualize the structure and operation of context-free grammars, experiment with different grammar configurations to 
see how changes affect parsing and parse tree construction, and develop a deeper understanding of the concepts of grammar rules, 
parsing, and syntax trees.
